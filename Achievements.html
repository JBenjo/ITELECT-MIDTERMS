Our Achievements

Celebrating the milestones and success stories that have shaped Batangas' manufacturing landscape

Batangas Milestones

First Natural Dyeing Facility

The establishment of the Philippines' first natural dyeing facility for textiles and handicrafts in Bauan, Batangas has been a game-changer for local artisans. This facility supports sustainable practices by using plant-based dyes derived from local sources.

The facility has empowered over 200 local artisans and created a new market for eco-friendly textiles, showcasing Batangas' commitment to preserving traditional crafts while embracing sustainable innovation.

Mango and Calamansi Processing Facility

The opening of a state-of-the-art mango and calamansi processing facility in Tanauan City has significantly enhanced the value of local agricultural products. This facility has reduced post-harvest losses and created new market opportunities for farmers.

By implementing advanced processing techniques, the facility has been able to produce high-quality preserves, juices, and essential oils that meet international standards, opening doors to export markets across Asia and beyond.

D&L Industries' LEED Gold Certified Plant

The PHP10.5-billion manufacturing plant established by D&L Industries in Batangas represents a significant advancement in the province's manufacturing capabilities. The facility has achieved LEED Gold Certification, reflecting its commitment to environmental responsibility.

This world-class facility employs the innovative "pack at source" strategy, allowing for the processing and packaging of raw materials closer to their source, enhancing efficiency and product quality while reducing the carbon footprint.

Success Stories

Mira's Turmeric Products: From Home Kitchen to Market Leader

A journey of innovation and community impact

The Beginning

What started as a home-based venture in a small kitchen in Lipa City has evolved into one of Batangas' most recognized brands of herbal products. Mira Santos began experimenting with turmeric-based remedies using traditional recipes passed down through generations of her family.

Growth and Innovation

With initial success in local markets, Mira invested in research to improve product formulation and shelf life. Collaborating with the Department of Science and Technology, she developed standardized production methods that maintained the potency of natural ingredients while ensuring consistent quality.

Community Impact

Today, Mira's Turmeric Products sources raw materials from over 50 small-scale organic farmers in Batangas, providing stable income and promoting sustainable farming practices. The company employs 35 local residents and has established a training program for women entrepreneurs interested in natural product manufacturing.

Key Success Factors

  • Unwavering commitment to product quality and natural ingredients
  • Strategic partnerships with local government and academic institutions
  • Responsive approach to customer feedback and market trends
  • Investment in sustainable packaging and eco-friendly production methods
  • Strong community ties and social responsibility initiatives

Manufacturing Processes

Traditional Meets Modern

Our manufacturing processes honor traditional techniques while embracing modern technology. This unique blend allows us to maintain the authenticity and quality of our natural products while improving efficiency and consistency.

For example, our Kapeng Barako production still involves careful hand-selection of coffee cherries, but utilizes modern roasting equipment with precise temperature controls to ensure consistent flavor profiles.

Similarly, our coconut oil extraction combines traditional cold-pressing methods with modern filtration systems to produce pure, high-quality virgin coconut oil without compromising its natural benefits.

Pack at Source Strategy

One of our innovative approaches is the "pack at source" strategy implemented by facilities like D&L Industries' Batangas plant. This approach allows for the processing and packaging of raw materials closer to their source, providing numerous benefits:

  • Reduced transportation costs and carbon emissions
  • Fresher ingredients leading to higher quality products
  • Support for local agricultural communities
  • Greater traceability and quality control
  • Reduced post-harvest losses

This strategy has been particularly effective for our herbal and fruit-based products, ensuring that the active compounds and flavors are preserved at their peak.

Sustainability Practices

Eco-Friendly Production

Our manufacturing facilities are designed to minimize environmental impact. We utilize renewable energy sources, implement water recycling systems, and optimize energy efficiency throughout our operations.

D&L Industries' Batangas plant has achieved LEED Gold Certification, a testament to its sustainable design and operation. The facility incorporates solar panels, rainwater harvesting, and advanced waste management systems.

Responsible Sourcing

We work closely with local farmers and suppliers who share our commitment to sustainable practices. Our sourcing policies prioritize organic farming methods, fair labor practices, and biodiversity conservation.

By establishing long-term relationships with our suppliers, we can ensure consistent quality while supporting the economic development of rural communities in Batangas.

Carbon Neutrality Goals

In alignment with Batangas City's initiative to become the first carbon-neutral city in the Philippines by 2030, our manufacturers are implementing comprehensive carbon reduction strategies.

These include transitioning to renewable energy, optimizing logistics to reduce transportation emissions, and investing in carbon offset projects such as reforestation in the Batangas region.

Quality Assurance

Our Commitment to Excellence

Quality is at the heart of everything we do. Our comprehensive quality assurance program ensures that every product meets the highest standards of safety, efficacy, and consistency.

We adhere to international standards and certifications, including ISO 9001, HACCP, and GMP. Our facilities undergo regular audits and inspections to maintain these certifications and ensure continuous improvement.

From raw material selection to final packaging, every step of our production process is monitored and documented to ensure traceability and quality control.

Our Quality Control Process

A multi-layered approach to ensure excellence

  • 1

    Raw Material Inspection

    Rigorous testing of all incoming materials for purity, potency, and contamination

  • 2

    In-Process Testing

    Continuous monitoring during production to ensure consistency and quality

  • 3

    Final Product Analysis

    Comprehensive testing of finished products for safety, efficacy, and compliance with specifications

  • 4

    Stability Testing

    Long-term studies to ensure product quality throughout shelf life

  • 5

    Continuous Improvement

    Regular review of processes and outcomes to identify opportunities for enhancement
